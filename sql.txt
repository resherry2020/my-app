 <td>Homosalate</td>
                <td>
                  Homosalate is a chemical compound commonly used in sunscreens
                  as an ultraviolet (UV) filter. Its primary function is to
                  absorb UVB radiation, providing protection against sunburn.
                  Homosalate is part of a group of chemicals known as chemical
                  UV filters.
                </td>
                <td>
                  Chemical UV filters have been associated with coral bleaching
                  and the disruption of coral ecosystems. There is evidence that
                  homosalate and similar compounds may have toxic effects on
                  certain marine species. For example, they might interfere with
                  the endocrine systems of marine organisms, affecting their
                  reproductive and developmental processes.
